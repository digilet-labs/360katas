Why is it named 360Katas

Kata (Japanese origin) is a way to gain mastery by deliberate practice. Our goal is not just to enable users to receive 360 degree feedback but also to help you set your development goals and move towards them with dedicated effort. Hence, the name :)

What’s the team behind 360Katas?

I heard that 360 degree feedback doesn’t work. How are you different?

Yes. You heard it right. In our opinion, 360 degree feedback has the following shortcomings:

- It defeats the purpose of getting honest feedback when clubbed with appraisals. It should be used only for discovering personal development goals
- Users can’t seek clarifications on any anonymous feedback received.
- Feedback should only be visible to the seeker and no one else, including the HR in the organisation.
- An intervention to act on the received feedback is missing! Without this, we don’t purposefully move towards being our best self!

360Katas has been designed to solve these specific shortcomings!


If added to a Slack workspace, can 360Katas see information about or messages in any channel?

360Katas does not request permission to view information about any channels that are present in your workspace and cannot view any messages apart from the direct messages sent to 360Katas. Even if added to any channel, 360Katas cannot view any messages, including when @360Katas is mentioned, and hence does not respond to any messages in channels!


How does the ‘Seek Clarification’ process safeguard anonymity?

When a user seeks clarification on a feedback by pressing ‘Seek Clarification’, 360Katas will identify the person who gave that feedback, and sends the seek clarification request without notification. Sometimes, if a user seeks clarification and a colleagues’ app rings a notification, coincidentally at the same time, one may try to associate that feedback with the colleague. Silent notification may help to protect anonymity in such situations.


I am worried that anonymity is compromised if the sender seeks feedback only from one person.

If a user sends a feedback request to only one person, and when s/he replies, the seeker will know the respondent. To retain anonymity, 360Katas makes it mandatory to seek feedback from at least 2 people. Moreover, the names of all respondents are displayed to every respondent.
